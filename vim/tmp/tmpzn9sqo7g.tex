Let $ABC$ be a scalene triangle with circumcircle $\Omega$, and suppose the incircle of $\triangle ABC$ touches $\seg{BC}$ at $D$. The angle bisector of $\angle A$ meets $\seg{BC}$ and $\Omega$ at $E$ and $F$. The circumcircle of $\triangle DEF$ intersects the $A$-excircle at $S_1$, $S_2$, and $\Omega$ at $T\ne F$. Prove that line $AT$ passes through either $S_1$ or $S_2$.

---

Let $T'$ be the $A$-mixtillinear touchpoint.

Since $\da BAT=\da DCT$ and $\da ATB=\da CTD$, we have $\triangle ABT\sim\triangle CDT$. Then
\[\da T'FE=\da T'FA=\da T'BA=\da T'DC=\da T'DE,\]
so $T'\in(DEF)$. Therefore $T=T'$.
\end{proof}

Now let $\seg{AT}$ intersect $(DEF)$ again at $S$. I will show $S$ lies on the $A$-excircle, and the conclusion will follow.

Let $\Psi$ denote inversion at $A$ with radius $\sqrt{AB\cdot AC}$, followed by reflection over the angle bisector of $\angle A$. Since $AS\cdot AT=AE\cdot AF=AB\cdot AC$, the image $S'$ of $S$ under $\Psi$ is the reflection of $T$ over the angle bisector of $\angle A$; in particular, $S'$ lies on the mixtillinear incircle.

But it is well-known that $\Psi$ swaps the $A$-excircle and the $A$-mixtillinear incircle, so $S$ lies on the excircle, the end.
